Remove the excess amount

If you over-contribute to your Tax-free Savings Account (TFSA) and the excess amount is still in your account, withdraw it as soon as possible. Any excess amount in your TFSA is taxable at 1% per month for as long as it remains in your account.

After you withdraw the excess amount, you do not need to contact the CRA. Your financial institution will send us proof of the withdrawal.

For more information: Withdrawing from a TFSA

How we notify you of an excess amount

The CRA will notify you if you have over-contributed to your TFSA. We will typically send this notification through your CRA account or by mail when we are informed of the excess by your financial institution, usually in late spring.

How TFSA issuers update the CRA

TFSA issuers must submit a summary of all the transactions that a holder makes in a calendar year by early sping of the next calendar year.

It is therefore possible for your TFSA to have an excess amount for several months before you are notified about it.

It is important to keep and maintain your own records, and to track your contributions closely. You should withdraw any excess amount as soon as possible to minimize penalties.

The notification provides information about the over-contribution, its impact on your available TFSA contribution room and the potential tax implications.

For more information: What is contribution room

If you are a non-resident

The CRA will send TFSA holders who are non-residents a notice of assessment (NOA) that details the excess amount in their account. It will show when the excess amount began, how long it was in the account and the date we processed your excess tax assessment.

If applicable, the NOA will also provide a detailed calculation of the tax that you owe on contributions you made to your TFSA while you were not a resident of Canada.

For more information:

  • If you are taxed on non-resident contributions
  • How non-residency affects your TFSA

If you owe tax due to an over-contribution

Any excess amount in your TFSA is subject to a 1% monthly tax. If you have an excess amount in your account, withdraw it as soon as possible.

To pay tax on the excess amount, refer to:

  • If you have to pay tax on a TFSA
  • If you are taxed on excess amounts

Requesting a tax waiver or cancellation

The CRA may waive or cancel all or part of the taxes if we determine that it is fair to do so. We will review each situation individually.

For more information: If you disagree with your assessment
